Who I Am

Beyond the Resume

What pulled me into DevOps was the architecture. The first time I understood how a CI/CD pipeline actually works end-to-end, how a commit triggers a build, runs tests, packages a container, and ships to production with no human touching it, I wanted to build more of that.

Then Kubernetes clicked. The way it handles scheduling, self-healing, rollbacks, and resource allocation across nodes. It's a genuinely interesting system to understand, and an even better one to run in production when you know what's happening under the hood.

That curiosity is why I'm here. Every role has been a chance to go deeper: more complex pipelines, tighter security guardrails, better observability. I build solutions because I want to understand how things actually work.

My MS in Computer Engineering gave me the foundation. Production systems gave me the rest. There's a version of engineering you learn in school, and a different one you learn when rollbacks have to work at 2am and someone's watching.

I care about the rollback as much as the deploy. Anyone can ship. The interesting part is making sure you can always get back to a good state, fast, without a runbook that's six months out of date.

AWS Certified Solutions Architect. Based in NJ. Interested in teams building infrastructure that actually has to stay up.
